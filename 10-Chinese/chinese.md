Welcome to your Chinese grammar notebook! Use this as a reference for HSK 1-2 level grammar and essential vocabulary.

## 🔷 GRAMMAR RULES

### Structure: Subject + Verb + Object (SVO)

**规则 (guīzé) - Rule:**
In Chinese, the basic sentence word order follows: Subject (主语) → Verb (谓语) → Object (宾语)
This is the same as English word order!

**例子 (lìzi) - Example:**
```
Wǒ chī fàn.
我 吃 饭。
I eat rice. / I'm eating rice.
```

**NB:**
- Chinese doesn't use spaces between words in writing
- "吃" (chī) means "to eat" - it's the same verb for all subjects!
- Time expressions often come at the beginning or end of the sentence

---

### Structure: Using 是 (shì) - "to be"

**规则:**
Use 是 (shì) to connect a subject with a description/noun. It's like "am/is/are" in English.

**例子:**
```
Wǒ shì xuéshēng.
我是学生。
I am a student.

Tā shì lǎoshī.
她是老师。
She is a teacher.
```

**NB:**
- 是 is NOT used with adjectives like "I'm happy" → Use 我很高兴 (wǒ hěn gāoxìng)
- Don't say 我是学生 (wrong) when describing what you DO, only what you ARE

---

### Structure: Making Questions with 吗 (ma)

**规则:**
Add 吗 at the END of a sentence to turn it into a yes/no question.

**例子:**
```
Nǐ hǎo ma?
你好吗?
Are you well? / How are you?

Nǐ xuéxí zhōngwén ma?
你学习中文吗?
Do you study Chinese?
```

**NB:**
- The tone of your voice (rising) can also indicate a question in spoken Chinese
- Answer with 是 (shì) for yes or 不 (bù) for no

---

### Structure: Using 很 (hěn) - "very/quite"

**规则:**
很 (hěn) is an adverb meaning "quite/very" that's often used before adjectives.
In simple sentences, it doesn't mean "very" - it's just a桥梁 (bridge) to make the sentence complete.

**例子:**
```
Wǒ hěn gāoxìng.
我很高兴。
I am happy.

Tā hěn máng.
他很忙。
He is busy.
```

**NB:**
- In HSK level 1-2, "我很高兴" just means "I am happy" (not "I am VERY happy")
- The word 很 makes the sentence feel more complete

---

### Structure: Negation with 不 (bù) and 没 (méi)

**规则:**
- 不 (bù) = not (for actions and current states)
- 没 (méi) = not/don't have (for past actions or possessions)

**例子:**
```
Wǒ bù xuéxí zhōngwén.
我不学习中文。
I don't study Chinese.

Wǒ méi yǒu qián.
我没钱。
I don't have money. (I don't have any money)
```

**NB:**
- 不 changes to bú when followed by a 4th tone syllable!
- 没 (méi) is used for "don't have" and completed actions in the past

---

### Structure: Expressing Existence with 有 (yǒu)

**规则:**
有 (yǒu) means "to have" or "there is/are"

**例子:**
```
Wǒ yǒu yì běn shū.
我有一本书。
I have a book.

Jīntiān yǒu hěn duō gōngzuò.
今天有很多工作。
There is a lot of work today.
```

**NB:**
- 有 is used to show possession ("I have...")
- There is/are: 有人 (yǒu rén) = there is someone, 没钱 (méi qián) = no money

---

### Structure: Numbers + Measure Words

**规则:**
In Chinese, you MUST use a measure word (量词 liàngcí) between a number and a noun.

**常用 measure words:**
- 个 (gè) - for people, things, ideas
- 本 (běn) - for books
- 张 (zhāng) - for flat things (paper, tables)
- 条 (tiáo) - for long thin things (fish, rivers)

**例子:**
```
yí gè rén
一个人
one person

sān běn shū
三本书
three books

liǎng zhāng zhǐ
两张纸
two pieces of paper
```

**NB:**
- This is one of the hardest points for English speakers!
- Start memorizing measure words alongside each new noun

## 🔑 ESSENTIAL VOCABULARY

### 是 (shì) - to be / am / is / are

```
Nǐ shì nǎ guó rén?
你是哪国人?
What country are you from?

Wǒ shì Měiguó rén.
我是美国人。
I am American.
```

---

### 吗 (ma) - (question particle)

```
Nǐ hǎo ma?
你好吗?
How are you?

Tā zài ma?
她在吗?
Is she there?
```

---

### 好 (hǎo) - good / well

```
Nǐ hǎo!
你好!
Hello! (Greeting)

Hǎo, xièxie!
好，谢谢!
Okay, thank you!
```

---

### 不 (bù) - not / no

```
Wǒ bù zhīdào.
我不知道。
I don't know.

Bù, bù shì!
不，不是!
No, it's not!
```

---

### 有 (yǒu) - to have / there is

```
Nǐ yǒu shíjiān ma?
你有时间吗?
Do you have time?

Jīntiān yǒu kè.
今天有课。
There is class today.
```

---

### 没 (méi) - don't have / not (past)

```
Wǒ méi qián.
我没钱。
I don't have money.

Wǒ méi chī fàn.
我没吃饭。
I didn't eat (rice/food).
```

---

### 很 (hěn) - quite / very

```
Wǒ hěn xǐhuan Chīnwén.
我很喜欢的。
I really like Chinese.

Tā hěn kěài.
她很可爱。
She is cute/adorable.
```

---

### 我 (wǒ) - I / me

```
Wǒ jiào...
我叫...
My name is...

Wǒ shì xuéshēng.
我是学生。
I am a student.
```

---

### 你 (nǐ) - you

```
Nǐ hǎo!
你好!
Hello! (to one person)

Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi?
你叫什么名字?
What is your name?
```

---

### 他/她/它 (tā/tā/tā) - he / she / it

```
Tā shì lǎoshī.
她是老师。
She is a teacher.

Tā hěn gāoxìng.
他很高兴。
He is happy.
```

---

### 什么 (shénme) - what

```
Nǐ jiào shénme?
你叫什么?
What is your name?

Nǐ xuéxí shénme?
你学习什么?
What do you study?
```

---

### 哪 (nǎ) - which

```
Nǐ shì nǎ guó rén?
你是哪国人?
Which country are you from?

Nǎ gè?
哪个?
Which one?
```

---

### 这/那 (zhè/nà) - this / that

```
Zhè shì shū.
这是书。
This is a book.

Nà shì lǎoshī.
那是老师。
That is a teacher.
```
