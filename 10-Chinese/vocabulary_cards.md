# 📇 Essential Vocabulary Cards & Grammar

## 📚 HSK Grammar Points

### 也 (yě) - also/too

**规则 (guīzé) - Rule:**
也 (yě) means "also" or "too" and comes after the subject but before the verb or adjective.

**例子 (lìzi) - Example:**
```
Wǒ yě shì xuéshēng.
我也 是 学生。
I am also a student.

Tā yě xǐhuan píngguǒ.
她也 喜欢 苹果。
She also likes apples.
```

**NB:**
- Place 也 after the subject and before the verb/adjective
- Can be used with 是 (shì) and other verbs
- Similar to English "also" placement

---

### 都 (dōu) - all/both

**规则:**
都 (dōu) means "all" or "both" and refers to all members of a group. It comes after the subject but before the verb.

**例子:**
```
Wǒmen dōu xǐhuan Zhōngwén.
我们 都 喜欢 中文。
We all like Chinese.

Tāmen dōu shì lǎoshī.
他们 都 是 老师。
They are all teachers.
```

**NB:**
- Used with plural subjects (我们, 你们, 他们, etc.)
- Can also be used with 时间 words: 我每天都学习 (I study every day)

---

### 但是 (dànshì) - but

**规则:**
但是 (dànshì) means "but" and is used to connect two contrasting statements.

**例子:**
```
Wǒ xǐhuan chá, dànshì wǒ bù xǐhuan kāfēi.
我喜欢茶，但是我不喜欢咖啡。
I like tea, but I don't like coffee.

Tīntiān hěn rè, dànshì wǒmen yě kěyǐ yóuyǒng.
今天很热，但是我们也可以游泳。
Today is very hot, but we can also swim.
```

**NB:**
- Similar to English "but" in placement
- Can be shortened to 不过 (bùguò) in casual speech

---

### 和 (hé) - and

**规则:**
和 (hé) means "and" and is used to connect nouns or noun phrases.

**例子:**
```
Wǒ hé tāmen shì péngyou.
我 和 他们 是 朋友。
I and they are friends. (We are friends)

Chá hé shuǐ dōu shì yǐnliào.
茶 和 水 都是 饮料。
Tea and water are both beverages.
```

**NB:**
- Primarily connects nouns, not verbs or adjectives
- For verbs, use 并且 (qiě) or just place actions sequentially
- In lists of more than two items, 和 is only used before the last item: 苹果、香蕉和橙子 (apples, bananas, and oranges)

---

### 因为...所以... (yīnwèi...suǒyǐ...) - because...so...

**规则:**
因为...所以... expresses cause and effect. 因为 introduces the reason, 所以 introduces the result.

**例子:**
```
Yīnwèi wǒ hěn máng, suǒyǐ wǒ bù néng qù.
因为 我 很 忙，所以 我 不 能 去。
Because I am busy, so I cannot go.

Tā yīnwèi xǐhuan Zhōngwén, suǒyǐ tā měitiān xuéxí.
她 因为 喜欢 中文，所以 她 每天 学习。
Because she likes Chinese, so she studies every day.
```

**NB:**
- Both parts are needed for the full pattern
- In casual speech, sometimes just 所以 is used: 我很忙，所以我不去。
- The order is always: 因为 [reason], 所以 [result]

---

### 如果...就... (rúguǒ...jiù...) - if...then...

**规则:**
如果...就... expresses conditional statements. 如果 introduces the condition, 就 introduces the result.

**例子:**
```
Rúguǒ nǐ yǒu shíjiān, jiù lái wǒ jiā.
如果 你 有 时间，就 来 我 家。
If you have time, then come to my house.

Rúguǒ tiān qì hǎo, jiù wǒmen qù gōngyuán.
如果 天气 好，就 我们 去 公园。
If the weather is good, then we will go to the park.
```

**NB:**
- 就 can sometimes be omitted in casual speech: 如果你有时间，来我家。
- The word 就 emphasizes that the result follows naturally from the condition

---

### 比 (bǐ) - than (for comparisons)

**规则:**
比 (bǐ) is used to make comparisons: A 比 B + adjective = A is more [adjective] than B.

**例子:**
```
Wǒ bǐ tā gāo.
我 比 他 高。
I am taller than he is.

Zhè běn shū bǐ nà běn shū guì.
这 本 书 比 那 本 书 贵。
This book is more expensive than that book.

Tāmen de gōngzuò bǐ wǒde máng.
他们 的 工作 比 我 的 忙。
Their work is busier than mine.
```

**NB:**
- The structure is: [A] 比 [B] + [adjective]
- No need for 更 (gèng) or 最 (zuì) in this construction
- To say "less than," use 不比 (bù bǐ) or 不是比 (bùshì bǐ)

---

### 最 (zuì) - most/-est

**规则:**
最 (zuì) means "most" or "-est" and is placed before an adjective to form the superlative.

**例子:**
```
Zhè ge píngguǒ zuì dà.
这个 苹果 最 大。
This apple is the biggest.

Wǒ zuì xǐhuan chī miàntiáo.
我 最 喜欢 吃 面条。
I like eating noodles the most.

Jīntiān shì zuì rè de yītiān.
今天 是 最 热 的 一 天。
Today is the hottest day.
```

**NB:**
- Structure: [Subject] + 最 + [adjective]
- Can be used with 的 (de) when modifying a noun: 最有趣的电影 (the most interesting movie)
- Opposite: 最不... (zuì bù...) for "least..."

---

### 又...又... (yòu...yòu...) - both...and...

**规则:**
又...又... is used to describe something that has two qualities or does two actions simultaneously.

**例子:**
```
Zhè ge píngguǒ yòu dà yòu tián.
这个 苹果 又 大 又 甜。
This apple is both big and sweet.

Wǒ jīntiān yòu máng yòu lèi.
我 今天 又 忙 又 累。
I am both busy and tired today.

Tā shuō de huà yòu duō yòu kuài.
她 说 的 话 又 多 又 快。
She talks both a lot and fast.
```

**NB:**
- Structure: 又 [adjective/verb] 又 [adjective/verb]
- Both qualities/actions are presented as equally true
- Can be used with adjectives, verbs, or verb phrases

---

### 一边...一边... (yìbiān...yìbiān...) - to do one thing while doing another

**规则:**
一边...一边... expresses doing two actions simultaneously, one after the other in the phrase.

**例子:**
```
Tā yībiān chī fàn, yībiān kàn shū.
他 一 边 吃 饭，一 边 看 书。
He eats while reading a book.

Wǒmen yībiān zǒu lù, yībiān tán tiān.
我们 一 边 走 路，一 边 聊 天。
We walk while chatting.

Nǐ kěyǐ yībiān tīng yīnyuè, yībiān zuò zuòyè.
你 可以 一 边 听 音乐，一 边 做 作业。
You can listen to music while doing homework.
```

**NB:**
- Structure: 一边 [action 1], 一边 [action 2]
- Both actions happen at the same time
- The verb 边 (biān) means "side" - literally "one side...the other side..."
