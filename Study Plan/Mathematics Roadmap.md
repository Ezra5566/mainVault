# English Mathematics Roadmap (Undergraduate Level)

## Overall Goals
- Systematically master core algebra, analytic geometry, calculus, and probability‑statistics
- Apply mathematical concepts to solve real-world problems and produce basic academic writing

## Time Planning
- Current Phase: Ongoing
- Short‑term Goals (0‑3 months): Foundations of algebra and mathematical reasoning
- Mid‑term Goals (3‑12 months): Calculus I (differential), Linear Algebra, and Probability Basics
- Long‑term Goals (1+ years): Advanced courses and a capstone project or research component

## Milestones
1. Complete algebraic manipulations and equation solving (2 weeks)
2. Master functions, limits, and continuity (3‑4 months)
3. Finish Calculus I – Differentiation and its applications (4‑6 months)
4. Complete Calculus II – Integration and applications (6‑9 months)
5. Finish Probability and Statistics fundamentals (9‑12 months)
6. Participate in math seminars, workshops, or a research project (12 months+)

## Recommended Resources
- Textbooks: *Calculus* (Stewart), *Linear Algebra Done Right* (Axler), *Probability and Statistics* (DeGroot & Schervish)
- Online Platforms: Khan Academy, MIT OpenCourseWare, 3Blue1Brown (YouTube)
- Practice Materials: *How to Prove It* (Velleman), problem sets from AoPS
- Visual Aids: 3Blue1Brown series on visual calculus and linear algebra

## Learning Methods
- Daily 45‑minute concept study and problem solving
- Weekly 1‑hour group problem‑solving session
- Monthly mini‑quizzes to reinforce knowledge
- Quarterly project or presentation to synthesize learning

## Tracking and Evaluation
- Monthly quizzes
- Semester‑end self‑assessment
- Feedback from instructors or peers
